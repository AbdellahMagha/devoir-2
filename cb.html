Tanjia

Tanjia (or tanjiya) is an iconic dish in Moroccan cuisine, originating from the city of Marrakech. It's a kind of meat stew, typically made with lamb or beef, marinated in a blend of traditional spices, then slowly cooked in a sealed clay pot.

couscous

Couscous is made from tiny granules of durum wheat or semolina, which are steamed until fluffy. It's often served with a stew of meat and vegetables.

Tajin

A tagine typically consists of slow-cooked meat (such as lamb, chicken, or beef) or fish, along with vegetables, fruits, and a blend of spices. Common ingredients include onions, tomatoes, garlic, olives, preserved lemons, and a variety of herbs and spices such as cumin, coriander, saffron, and cinnamon.
